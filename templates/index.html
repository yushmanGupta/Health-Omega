Brain Tumor

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain.It can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous)

Causes : In case of most brain tumors it is unknown, though genetics and radiation exposure can be factors.

Symptoms:headaches, seizures, nausea, and vision problems.

Treatment options: surgery, radiation therapy, and chemotherapy.

Remember that early diagnosis improves the chances of successful treatment.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the lungs. It is commonly caused by smoking, though non-smokers can also develop it.

Types:

  1. Adenocarcinoma
  2. Large cell carcinoma
  3. Squamous Cell Carcinoma

The symptoms causes and tratment depends on the location and type of cancer.Remember that early diagnosis improves the chances of successful treatment.

Kidney Cyst:

A kidney cyst is a fluid-filled sac that forms in the kidneys.Most kidney cysts are benign and do not cause symptoms or harm.

Kidney Tumor:

A kidney tumor is an abnormal growth in the kidney, which can be benign or cancerous.Symptoms may include blood in the urine, pain, and unexplained weight loss.

Kidney Stones:

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals that form in the kidneys.They can cause severe pain, especially during urination, and may require treatment to pass

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious infectious disease primarily affecting the lungs, caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Causes : Infection with Mycobacterium tuberculosis, spread through airborne droplets from an infected person.

Symptoms:Persistent cough, chest pain, weight loss, fever, and night sweats.

Treatment options: A combination of antibiotics, typically including isoniazid, rifampin, ethambutol, and pyrazinamide, taken over several months.
